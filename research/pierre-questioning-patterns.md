## Probing the Experience

| Pierre Says | Function |
|------------|----------|
| "What was that like?" | The quintessential Grimes question — asks for qualitative experience, not analysis |

## Deepening Questions

| Pierre Says | Function |
|------------|----------|
| "How did that happen?" | Asks about mechanism — not 'why' but 'how' |
| "What is taking the action?" | THE core Socratic question — identifies the Self behind action |
| "What's looking? What's asking? What's deciding?" | Chain of recursive self-inquiry |
| "In respect to any image of the self, what would you say?" | Tests for ego/image involvement |
| "In the moment of decision itself, or immediately after?" | Precise temporal discrimination |

## Release & Reframe

| "That's a rather interesting state of mind, isn't it?" | Invites wonder rather than analysis |

| Pierre Says | Function |

## Challenging Resistance

## Key Observations for Prompt Design

### 1. Pierre Asks ONE Question at a Time
Never stacks questions. Each question is a complete unit. The AI must do the same.

### 2. Pierre Lets Silence Happen
When the dreamer says "Hmm" or goes quiet (03:16, 03:30), Pierre waits. He doesn't fill the space. The AI must resist the urge to fill silence with more text.

### 3. Pierre Redirects to the Question
When the dreamer goes intellectual or deflects, Pierre calmly restates his question. He doesn't chase the deflection. Pattern:
- Dreamer: "Well, that's the turning point of the dream, but that doesn't answer your question."
- Pierre: "Right. That's important."
- [Pause]
- Pierre: "Are you saying there's nothing that you can describe in the dream at that moment?"

### 4. Pierre Uses the Dreamer's Language
He mirrors exact words back: "You said 'fleeting' — let me go back to it." The AI should always use the dreamer's vocabulary.

### 5. Pierre Lets Insights Emerge Without Naming Them
The dreamer says "It's a mystery" and Pierre doesn't explain it. The dreamer says "I don't want to put a label on it" and Pierre doesn't force one. He waits until the dreamer is ready.

### 6. Pierre Connects Dreams to Previous Sessions
"You know, we've had similar dreams like this in the past." This is where session memory becomes critical — the AI should reference previous dreams and insights.

### 7. Pierre Distinguishes Before/During/After
The major insight of this session: there's fear BEFORE the action, nothing DURING the action (ego-free), and description AFTER. Pierre helps the dreamer see this three-part structure.

### 8. The "What Is It?" Question
"What is taking the action?" is THE philosophical question. It's not rhetorical — Pierre genuinely asks it. The AI should use this question when the dreamer describes spontaneous, appropriate action without ego involvement.
